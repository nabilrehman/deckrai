# Test Case 2: Nike (Consumer Brand - Different Industry)

## Test Parameters

**Company Name:** Nike

**Content/Narrative:**
Athlete endorsement campaign presentation for the launch of a new Nike running shoe line featuring professional marathon runner Sarah Chen. The deck should showcase:
- Sarah's journey from amateur to Olympic qualifier
- The role of Nike in her training and success
- Technical innovations in the new shoe line
- Campaign creative concepts (photos, videos, social media)
- Launch timeline and expected market impact

The presentation should cover:
1. Athlete spotlight: Sarah Chen's inspiring story
2. The innovation: New shoe technology and features
3. The campaign: Creative concepts across channels
4. The reach: Target demographics and market strategy
5. The launch: Timeline, events, partnerships
6. Expected outcomes: Sales projections, brand lift

**Target Audience:** Nike marketing executives, brand managers, campaign stakeholders

**Presentation Goal:** Get buy-in for the athlete endorsement campaign, showcase the compelling athlete story, demonstrate strategic thinking, inspire confidence in launch success

**Desired Slide Count:** 10-12 slides

---

## Expected Quality Baseline

This test evaluates the prompt's ability to handle:
- **Consumer Brand** (vs. B2B enterprise like SolarWinds/Atlassian)
- **Emotional/Story-Driven Content** (vs. data/metrics-driven)
- **Bold, Athletic Visual Style** (vs. professional corporate)

### Brand Research Quality (10 points)
- Find Nike's iconic black #111111 and white #FFFFFF palette
- Discover Nike orange (Swoosh orange) if used in recent branding
- Identify typography (likely Futura, Trade Gothic, or custom Nike typeface)
- Extract brand personality (bold, inspiring, athletic, empowering, "Just Do It")
- Recognize Nike's minimalist, hero-image-driven design language

### Visual Hierarchy Clarity (10 points)
- Strong image-led hierarchy (70-80% visual weight for hero photos)
- Emotional focal points (athlete's face, action shots)
- Minimal text (even less than B2B decks)
- Bold, impactful layout choices

### Architecture Detail (10 points)
- Full-bleed photo treatments specified
- Image overlay treatments documented
- Typography on images (contrast, shadows, overlays)
- Dramatic layouts (not conservative grids)

### Specifications Completeness (10 points)
- Nike brand colors exact
- Photo treatment specs (overlays, filters, crops)
- Typography hierarchy for consumer/emotional content
- Icon style (likely filled, bold, minimal)

### Design System Quality (10 points)
- Consumer brand design language captured
- Photography style guidelines detailed
- Emotional vs. rational design balance
- Campaign aesthetic vs. corporate aesthetic

---

## Success Criteria

**Minimum Passing Score:** 45/50 (90%)

**Specific Checks:**
- [ ] Nike brand recognized as minimalist, bold, image-driven
- [ ] Hero images dominate layouts (70-80% visual weight)
- [ ] Typography is bold and impactful (not corporate)
- [ ] "Just Do It" energy captured in design language
- [ ] Athlete story is visual-first, text-minimal
- [ ] Full-bleed photo treatments specified
- [ ] Design rationale reflects consumer/emotional goals
- [ ] Campaign aesthetic distinguished from corporate decks

---

## Additional Evaluation Criteria

**Adaptability Test:**
- Can the prompt handle a radically different brand personality?
- Does it recognize consumer vs. enterprise design differences?
- Does it adapt visual hierarchy for emotional content?
- Does it specify photo-driven layouts vs. icon/diagram-driven?

**Expected Differences from SolarWinds:**
- More visual weight to images (70-80% vs. 50-60%)
- Less text overall (5-8 words per slide vs. 10-15)
- Bolder layouts (full-bleed, asymmetric, dramatic)
- Emotional focal points (faces, action) vs. data focal points (charts, stats)
- Inspirational tone vs. professional tone
